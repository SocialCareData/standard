## Summary

Supporting access and contribution to a shared care record (from the data subject and family networks) in a way that accurately represents the person’s voice, experience and preferences.

### User Stories 

*	**Child/ young person contributing to their record**
    * As a child/ young person I need to be able to share my experiences and preferences and for this to be represented on my record 
* **Individual (adult) contributing to their record**
   * As an adult receiving care and support I need to access information about me, complete assessments, manage care records, and track progress to maintain control and independence. I need to update information about my preferences, routines, and communication needs, so that professionals understand how best to support me, and I need to approve, question, or correct information recorded about me, so that my record is accurate and reflects my perspective.
*	**Family network contributing to a record**
   * As a family member participating in a family group decision-making process, I need to be able to contribute towards decisions and plans made for a child or young person in a way that’s easily captured on a system, so that a record of my views and involvement can be made 
   * As a parent/ individual with parental responsibility, I need to be able to contribute towards the record of the person I have responsibility for, so that my experiences of caring for the person can be considered as part of the wider network of involvement 
   * As a carer, I need to contribute relevant observations or updates about the person I support, so that social workers and providers can make timely decisions.
* **Professional requirements to represent voice of the person**
   * As a social worker, I need to be able to capture the voice of the person I am supporting and their family members in their records in a way that is efficient, so that I can capture their voice without needing to double key lots of information.
   * As a social worker, I need CMS records that hold family members accountable for their agreed actions.


## Description

Both children’s and adults’ social workers are legally required to reflect the wishes and feelings of the people they support in care records. 

**For children and young people**, participation at an age-appropriate level empowers them and helps them to understand their context, even if they disagree with decisions being made to protect them.  This approach is both ethical and likely to lead to better outcomes for them.  

**For adults in receipt of care**, control over what data is held and the ability to update and add to a record is even more important. In Adult Social Care, people often move between services (e.g., hospital, mental health, community support, home care). Without shared records, social workers and partners must rely on manual enquiries, repeated questions, or incomplete historical information. This creates risk, inefficiency, and frustration for the person. Data subjects should be able to add or verify information about what matters to them, their goals, routines, and preferences, which are core to Care Act–compliant assessments and person-centred support planning.

### THE CHALLENGE / OPPORTUNITY

#### Primary challenge
	
* The voice of the child/family always needs to go through the social worker. 
* CMS’ do not consistently support non-professional care actors from contributing towards a record 
* The nature of their contribution is generally limited to free text, which is written by the social worker after speaking with the child/individual. This limits ‘true’ capture of a child/individual’s voice.  It is often recorded as part of a (for example) home visit record, making it difficult to locate and collate later.
* This ‘double keying’ can be time intensive for the social worker, and dilutes the ‘user voice’ as in their write up the social worker might omit/alter certain points based on their unintended biases, or simply because as a professional they have different priorities and focus points.
* Whilst technology exists that allows for children and families to input directly into their record (i.e. portals) it is not universally adopted and does not integrate well with case management systems. 
Secondary challenges
* Family Group Decision Making (FGDM) is not supported where the social worker holds control and power over what is recorded on the CMS.
* Current systems for coproduction are hard to use and difficult to navigate.
* Digital divide and accessibility issues: Family members may have accessibility needs, learning difficulties, language barriers, psychological challenges, or other personality traits that create barriers to them accessing and contributing to secure records (e.g. via two-factor authentication) In addition, some may not have access to the right tools e.g. laptops, consistent internet access.

#### Opportunity
* Enable integration of ‘family facing’ digital tools that can capture the voice of the child and family, whether they use written, text, voice, photos, videos or drawing.
* Only appropriate information from the child’s record will be available during sessions with families.
* Outputs from these tools are stored within the CMS in the correct place and can be easily retrieved.
* Children, family and network members can input into co-produced records as and when they need to.

### CARE RECORD COMPONENTS

A Care Record Component is a distinct unit of information that could be captured within the Case Management System (CMS). These components range from the structural entities required to safely manage a case to the person-centred details that form a holistic view of an individual's life and journey. Relevant care record components for this use case include:

* Record of experience – this might be structured feedback gathered at regular meeting such as a CP Conference or CLA Review, or in response to a significant interaction with a professional e.g. a social worker visit.
* Responses to specific assessments or plans – this might be to indicate agreement or disagreement with the professionals’ views and plans set out in these documents.  Should be included as part of the final version of the assessment or plan.
* Goals identified – usually a response to concerns raised by professionals, should be included alongside professionals’ plans especially when these differ.
* Preferences and communication needs
* Media records - using voice, video or image files to capture voice of children/individuals, integrated within the persons CMS record

### BENEFITS

When inputting in any form:

* Children and families’ input is taken into account when making decisions about them (including encouraging Family Group Decision Making processes)
* Individuals in receipt of care can see their records reflect their own voice and decisions

If able to directly input, then:

* Children, families and individuals in receipt of care have increased confidence that their voice is being heard and can see a direct link between this and the decisions that are made about them. 
* Local authorities can monitor and evidence the extent to which children are families are being directly consulted and involved in the work undertaken with them. 
* Social workers can use a variety of tools to capture the input from children/ individuals without having to duplicate effort (e.g. writing up and summarising an interview ). 
* Children and families’ views are stored in consistent and searchable form

## Out of scope

* Specific decisions about who is able to record what on a child’s case file are professional judgements that each social worker will want to make for themselves
* The balance between facilitating commenting on a plan vs coproducing a plan will also depend on the individual LA’s practice approach.
* Decisions about accessibility, although any user interface should have a high baseline of accessibility.
* Defining what should or should not be recorded / accessed by non-professionals; where / how that information should be stored; how content is moderated
