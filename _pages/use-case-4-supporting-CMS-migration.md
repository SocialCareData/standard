## Summary

**Generic:** As a local authority, we need to be able to easily move between case management systems and ensure they deliver maximum value for staff and public services, so that we can improve social worker effectiveness, ensure responsible use of public funds, and foster a more competitive and innovative CMS market.

**Examples**

As a social care leader, I need to be getting the best value for my social workers from their case management system so that they can do their jobs most effectively

As a social care leader, I need to be getting the best value for money from my case management system so that I can evidence sensible use of public funds

As a social care data lead, I need to be able to easily migrate data between my current system and newly commissioned systems so that social workers can continue working seamlessly between systems

As a social care leader, I need a range of software options available for purchase that will reduce the administrative burden on my social workers and admin staff, so that I can make the case to my council for spending more on software.

## Description

It is, by all accounts, an ordeal to transfer between case management systems. Commissioning cycles are long, local authorities generally get ‘locked in’ to one supplier and it is uncommon for a local authority to switch providers. When they do decide to switch, it takes many months of support from both the outgoing and incoming vendor to migrate data across, set up replicable workflows/forms and train new users.

We hypothesise that part of the reason why there is a lack of innovation in the market is due to this vendor ‘lock in’. Vendors know that local authorities are unable to easily move to another vendor, and the lack of competition in the market means that the business case to transfer between different vendors doesn’t easily stack up.

Part of the challenge here is the bespoke way in which systems are configured locally. Each instance of a system will be different in subtle ways and adapted to local contexts, making it difficult to ‘lift and shift’ elements of config and deploy this in another local authority.

It is extremely unlikely that vendors will do any work to support this use case, as it poses a substantial risk to their current market position. However, it is important to highlight it as something that users highlighted as a challenge when we engaged with them.

### Data

Everything in the case management system: config, cases, people, history, places. Everything will need to be migrated between systems

### Benefits/Goal/Value/Purpose

- Making it easier to transfer between systems will support competition within the CMS market, as vendors will have to  create innovative features that offer a better experience for social workers

### Processes

- This is an incredibly intensive process at the moment, taking many months (even years) to transfer between systems. Local authorities know this, so it is rare that this happens

## Landscape Review

Futuregov spent two years building a family-friendly CMS (Family Story) with the tri-borough in London, but were then unable to enter the market due to the barriers.
